## 10. Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Brand Voice Characteristics

**Professional**
- Use industry terminology appropriately
- Maintain formal structure in official communications
- Ensure accuracy in financial information

**Approachable**
- Avoid overly complex jargon
- Explain technical terms
- Use conversational language in marketing

**Trustworthy**
- Be transparent about processes and fees
- Acknowledge limitations honestly
- Provide clear, accurate information

**Confident**
- Use active voice
- Make clear, definitive statements
- Demonstrate expertise

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Website | Professional yet welcoming, informative, action-oriented |
| Marketing | Enthusiastic, customer-focused, results-oriented |
| Legal | Formal, precise, comprehensive, cautious |
| Customer Comms | Personal, clear, solution-focused, empathetic |

### Language Guidelines

**Do Use**:
- "We" and "our" for personal connection
- Active voice
- Concrete examples
- Benefits-focused language
- Inclusive terminology

**Avoid**:
- Passive voice (when possible)
- Excessive unexplained jargon
- Overpromising or guarantees
- Negative framing

### Writing Style
- Sentences: 15-20 words average
- Use bullet points for lists
- Bold key information
- Follow accessibility guidelines
